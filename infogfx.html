Counterterrorism and Counterproliferation

Sub-header

  • The U.S. government approach to reducing nuclear threats reflects the “prevent-counter-respond” framework to combat against current and emerging threats.
    • Prevent terrorists from developing nuclear capabilities or obtaining weapons-usable materials and technology;
    • Counter efforts by terrorists to acquire, develop, or smuggle these capabilities; and
    • Respond to nuclear incidents, whether by searching for and rendering safe a stolen foreign nuclear weapon or improvised nuclear device, or managing the consequences of a nuclear detonation, and conducting forensics and attribution activities.
  • Nuclear terrorism has evolved and the response must also evolve to meet emerging threats.
  • The U.S. will maintain the scientific, technical, and engineering capabilities to respond to nuclear terrorism and incidents involving nuclear or radiological threats.
